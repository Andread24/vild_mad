WHAT IS A FORRAGING?

Foraging is the act of searching for and gathering food. It's a behavior exhibited by animals, including humans, to find sustenance in nature. Foraging can involve searching for edible plants, fruits, nuts, roots, fungi, or hunting and gathering small animals or fish. It has been a fundamental activity throughout human history, and it's still practiced by some communities today.

WHERE CAN I DO IT

Foraging is like exploring a treasure map in real life! You can find goodies like berries, nuts, and even edible plants all around you. Look in places like forests, where you might find mushrooms or wild herbs. In open fields and meadows, there could be tasty wildflowers or herbs waiting to be picked. If you're near the coast, check out beaches and rocky shores for seaweed and shellfish. Even in cities, parks and abandoned lots might have edible plants like dandelions or fruit trees.
